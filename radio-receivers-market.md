<p><p>The radio receivers market includes various types, with the superheterodyne receiver being the most prevalent, known for its excellent selectivity and sensitivity. It converts radio frequencies to a lower fixed frequency for easier processing. Reflex receivers are a simpler design that uses fewer components, allowing for compactness and cost-effectiveness but at the expense of performance. Other types encompass a range of specialized designs, such as direct conversion receivers and software-defined radios, catering to diverse applications from amateur radio to commercial broadcasting.</p></p>
