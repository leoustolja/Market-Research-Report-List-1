<p><p>The inguinal hernia graft market is divided into two primary types: direct and indirect inguinal hernia grafts. Direct inguinal hernia grafts involve hernias that occur through a weakness in the abdominal wall, typically in older individuals. Indirect inguinal hernia grafts, on the other hand, occur through the inguinal canal, often seen in younger patients and present at birth. Both types require surgical intervention, and the graft market caters to varied surgical techniques and material preferences for effective repair.</p></p>
