<p><p>The aerosol delivery device market consists of three primary types: Dry Powder Inhalers (DPIs), Metered Dose Inhalers (MDIs), and nebulizers. DPIs deliver medication in powder form, requiring patients to inhale forcefully to activate the device. MDIs use a propellant to release a specific dose of medication as an aerosol spray, allowing for easier coordination of inhalation. Nebulizers convert liquid medication into a fine mist, enabling easier delivery for patients with respiratory difficulties, especially in pediatric and geriatric populations.</p></p>
