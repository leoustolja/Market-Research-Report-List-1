<p><p>The pericardial patch market is categorized into three main types: autologous, xenograft, and synthetic materials. Autologous patches utilize the patient's own tissue, enhancing biocompatibility and reducing rejection risk. Xenograft patches are derived from animal tissues, providing a viable alternative with suitable structural properties. Synthetic patches are made from artificial materials, offering durability and consistent performance, though they may carry a higher risk of complications. Each type caters to specific surgical needs and patient conditions, contributing to diverse treatment options.</p></p>
