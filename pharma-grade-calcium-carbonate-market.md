<p><p>Pharma grade calcium carbonate is categorized primarily into two types: Ground Calcium Carbonate (GCC) and Precipitated Calcium Carbonate (PCC). GCC is derived from natural limestone through coarse crushing and fine milling, resulting in a product with varied particle sizes, suitable for pharmaceutical applications. In contrast, PCC is synthetically produced through a chemical process, offering higher purity and uniformity. Both types serve as excipients in drug formulations, offering crucial roles in dosage form stability and bioavailability enhancement in the pharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
