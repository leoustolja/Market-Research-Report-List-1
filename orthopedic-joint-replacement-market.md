<p><p>The orthopedic joint replacement market is primarily classified into total and partial replacement types. Total joint replacement involves the complete removal of a damaged joint, such as the hip or knee, and its replacement with an artificial implant, restoring full function and alleviating pain. In contrast, partial joint replacement targets only the damaged portion of a joint, preserving healthier tissue and bone while still providing significant relief and mobility. Both options aim to enhance patients' quality of life and functional abilities.</p></p>
