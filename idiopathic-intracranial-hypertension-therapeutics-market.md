<p><p>The Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension (IIH) therapeutics market primarily includes Acetazolamide, Furosemide, Topiramate, and Octreotide. Acetazolamide, a carbonic anhydrase inhibitor, reduces cerebrospinal fluid production, alleviating pressure. Furosemide, a diuretic, helps in fluid management. Topiramate serves as both an anticonvulsant and a weight-loss aid, indirectly benefiting IIH patients. Octreotide, a somatostatin analog, may regulate cerebrospinal fluid dynamics. Collectively, these medications aim to reduce intracranial pressure and improve patient outcomes in IIH management.</p></p>
